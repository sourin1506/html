Nofollow Links

What Are Nofollow Links?

Nofollow links are links with a rel=”nofollow” HTML tag applied to them. The nofollow

tag tells search engines to ignore that link. Because nofollow links do not pass PageRank

they likely don’t impact search engine rankings.

Nofollow vs. Dofollow Links — What’s the Difference?

The only technical difference between the two is that a nofollow link has a nofollow tag.

< a href="https://www.w3schools.com/html/default.asp">

< a href="https://www.w3schools.com/html/default.asp" rel="nofollow">
Nofollow link – Technical

As a user, it’s impossible to tell the difference between a nofollow and dofollow link.

You can click on, copy and use a nofollow link like any other link on the web.

However, when it comes to search engine optimization, there’s a BIG difference between nofollow and dofollow links.
